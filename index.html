World Wide Web Information Document · Est. 1991

Welcome to the World Wide Web. This document contains information about the internet and how it is used to share text, data, and hyperlinked documents across the global network.

Jan '94

For decades, the internet was built around text. Documents were written, shared, and stored as words on a page. The hyperlink made it possible to connect one document to another, and the web was born.

Then images became the language of the web. Photographs, illustrations, and graphics began to fill pages that were once purely text. Software could read images, index them, and make them searchable.

Now, the world runs on video.

Available resources on this document server:

  • Text documents — fully indexed and searchable
  • Image libraries — browsable by filename and tag
  • Video files — stored, but the content is there. Software just can't reach it. _

But video is the hardest format for software to understand. Hours of footage sit in folders, unlabeled, unsearchable, and unreachable. The content is there. Software just can't reach it.
